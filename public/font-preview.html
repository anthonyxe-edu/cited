The evidence on omega-3 and cognitive health

EPA and DHA, the long-chain omega-3 fatty acids found in fatty fish and fish oil, have been the subject of extensive research in cognitive function. Multiple meta-analyses suggest a meaningful association between higher omega-3 intake and reduced risk of cognitive decline in middle age.

However, the picture is nuanced. While observational data is compelling, randomized controlled trials in cognitively healthy adults have shown modest or mixed results — suggesting omega-3s may be most protective as a preventive measure rather than a therapeutic one.
